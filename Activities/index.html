Diversity

A dialogue on diversity within the mathematics community is long overdue.

I am an inaugural MATCH Fellow of the program Matching Mathematicians with Classrooms Nationwide, organized by the American Institute of Mathematics and the Mathematical Association of America.

I serve in the committee for Equity, Diversity and Inclusion of the UCLA Department of Mathematics.

I (co)organize the California State University Math Summer Bridge Program, a joint effort between UCLA, Cal State LA, CSUN, and CSUSB.

I am a participant of the Latinx Mathematicians Research Community.

I (co)founded the Graduate Students Diversity Committee of the TAMU Department of Mathematics.

Directed Reading Program

I (co)organized the Directed Reading Program at Texas A&M University.

I was a mentor of the Directed Reading Program at Texas A&M University:

  • Fall 2019: together with Isaac Ray we read Symmetry: An Introduction to Group Theory and Its Applications by Roy McWeeny.
  • Spring 2019: together with Guadalupe Valles we read Affine Maps, Euclidean Motions and Quadrics by Agusti Reventos Tarrida.

I was a mentor of the Directed Reading Program at the University of California, Berkeley:

  • Spring 2020: together with Dmitriy Khripkov we read Traces in symmetric monoidal categories by Kate Ponto and Michael Shulman.

A collection of resources and ideas to help start and run a Directed Reading Program can be found at the DRP Network.
